Robot Encounter Study

This study investigates how people navigate shared spaces with robots. You will encounter a robot approaching you in a corridor and walk toward it.

The study takes approximately 8–12 minutes and consists of 8 short trials. There are no right or wrong responses — we are interested in your natural behaviour.

No personal data beyond anonymous interaction logs is collected. You may stop at any time by closing this window. By clicking Begin you consent to participate.

How it works

  • You will be placed in a first-person view of a corridor. Your character moves forward automatically.
  • A robot will approach you from the far end of the corridor.
  • Use A and D keys to steer left or right. Navigate however feels natural.
  • After each encounter you will answer four quick rating questions.
  • Some robots will look different from others. Some trials may include a sound — pay attention to it.

The yellow line on the floor marks the centre of the corridor. The coloured bar at the top shows how close the robot is.
